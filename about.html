About Me

My name is Edward Cordero. I'm an educator, researcher, and community engagement professional committed to empowering immigrant communities. With over 8 years of experience in teaching, outreach, and program coordination, I’ve worked across Latin America and New York City to promote education, health, and leadership among underrepresented communities.

Currently, I’m learning software development to build tools that connect people to life-changing resources. I believe technology should serve humanity—and I’m on a mission to build bridges of opportunity.

🔍 Experience Highlights

  • Coordinator of Community Engagement – Voces Latinas: Led outreach programs for immigrant Latinas in NYC.
  • Science Teacher – De La Salle Academy: Taught biology and chemistry with engaging strategies.
  • Researcher – UIS: Contributed to educational innovation and research.
  • Community Manager – National Agency for Overcoming Extreme Poverty: Coordinated services to support low-income communities and Supported communities affected by forced displacement.

🎓 Education

  • Bachelor’s in Social work, UDI
